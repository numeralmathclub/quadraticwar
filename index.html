Game Rules

Overview

Quadratic War is a two-player abstract strategy game that combines algebra and positional movement. Players arrange pieces to form linear or quadratic equations. If a valid equation (ax² + bx + c = 0) with real solutions is formed spanning both players' pieces, the opponent's pieces are removed.

Movement

  • Quadratic (x²): Moves up to 3 steps in any direction (Diagonal/Cardinal).
  • Linear (x): Moves up to 2 steps (Horizontal/Vertical only).
  • Constant (1): Moves 1 step forward only.

Winning

Eliminate all opponent pieces. If you lose all your pieces, you lose.

Equation Mechanic

After a move, the game checks for lines of pieces involved. If they form an equation ax² + bx + c = 0:

  • Real Solutions (Δ ≥ 0): Success! Opponent's pieces in the line are destroyed.
  • Complex Solutions (Δ < 0): Backfire! Your own pieces in the line are destroyed.
